.:: OCEP ::.

 

 

Thank you for your interest in the Department of Atomic Energy, India. Answers to some of the common queries are as follows:

  1. Why there is no mention of OCEP for MTech Applicants in the Advertisement this Year?
    OCEP is discontinued from this Year.

     
  2. Are following Degrees/ Disciplines eligible for OCES?
    BE (Ceramics/ Civil/ Production/ Information Tech)
    MCA, MSc (Computer Sc), B Pharma, BDS
    No.

     
  3. MSc qualification is as mentioned in the advertisement, however, subjects in BSc are not as required. Am I eligible?
    No.

     
  4. I have scored more than 60 % marks in MSc but failed to do so in BSc (or vice-versa). Can I apply for OCES/ DGFS?
     
    (or)

    I have scored decent percentile in GATE exam. But have scored 59.99 (or less) aggregate in BE final. Can I apply?
    No.

     

  5. How can I find % marks equivalent to CPI/CGPI?
    % Marks = (CPI awarded / 10) x 100 (where CPI maximum =10)
    Example: if CPI = 8.92 out of 10; % Marks = 89.2.

     
  6. I am an engineering graduate who has not appeared in GATE exam earlier and have not applied for coming GATE-2004 exam. Can I apply?
    No. GATE is must for OCES/ DGFS (Engg Discipline).

     
  7. I am an engineering graduate. I have a valid GATE-2003 score and I am also appearing/ have appeared for GATE-2004 exam. Should I apply now and submit my GATE-2004 Score later?
    You are requested to apply only after GATE-2004 score is available (using either GATE-2003 or GATE-2004 score whichever is higher).

     
  8. I satisfy all the educational criteria mentioned in advertisement but I am overage by 1 day or more as specified. Can I apply?
    No.

     
  9. What is the syllabus and other details for BARC Written Test for OCES/ DGFS (Science Discipline)?
    Written Test is of 2 hr duration with separate question papers for each discipline. It comprises l00 objective type questions broadly covering following subjects:
     
    Physics: BSc (Maths), BE (Engg Phys) and MSc (Physics & Electronics)
    Chemistry: StdXII (Maths), BSc (Physics) and MSc (Chemistry)
    Biosciences: StdXII (Maths), BSc (Physics & Chemistry) and MSc (all postgraduate specialisations of Bioscience mentioned in the Advertisement including MBBS)
    Environmental Science: StdXII (Maths), BSc (Physics & Chemistry) and MSc (Physics, Radiation Phys, Environmental Science)


    Table-1 Sample Questions

     

    1. Light emitting diode has a typical voltage drop of:
    A) 0.7V B) 0.3V C) 1.6V D) 1.02V
    2. The set of values for which x3+1 >= x2+x
    A) x <= 0 B) -1 <= x <= 1 C) x >= 0 D) x >= -1
    3) Most abundant chemical substance within a cell is:
    A) protein B) DNA C) carbohydrate D) water



    Each correct choice of answer will carry 3 marks and each wrong answer will carry 1 negative mark.
    ( <= should be read as "Less than or equal to"; >= should be read as "Greater than or equal to" )

     

  10. Can I appear in more than one paper in the Written Test?
    Written Test for Phy, Chem & RPES will be conducted simultaneously in one Session and for Bioscience in another Session on the same day Feb 22, 2004. MSc (Phy) and MSc (Chem) applicants should, therefore, be prepared for their Written Test Subject depending upon choice of the two OCES Training Disciplines as indicated in Table-5 (under comments marked (#)).
